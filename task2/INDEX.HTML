Pav Bhaji

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled and mashed
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers (capsicum), finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons Pav Bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Butter for cooking
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
  • 8 pav (soft dinner rolls)
  • Lemon wedges for serving


    Instructions: >Heat a large pan or griddle (tava) on medium-high heat. Add 2 tablespoons of butter. Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute or until the raw smell disappear. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent. Add finely chopped bell peppers and cook for a few minutes until they soften. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy. Add Pav Bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes. Add boiled and mashed potatoes, cauliflower, and green peas. Mix everything together. Mash the vegetables using a potato masher or the back of a ladle. Keep mashing and stirring until you get a uniform, thick mixture. You can add some water if it's too thick. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add more butter if you like. Cut the pav (dinner rolls) in half horizontally. Heat a griddle or pan and toast the pav with a little butter until they are lightly browned and crisp. Serve the hot and spicy Bhaji with the toasted pav, a dollop of butter, and lemon wedges on the side. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Enjoy your homemade Pav Bhaji!.

    Paneer Masala

    Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
    For the Gravy:
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
    Instructions:
  • In a bowl, mix yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt.
  • Add the cubed paneer to the marinade and coat it well. Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes. Making the Paneer Masala:
  • Heat oil or ghee in a pan or skillet over medium heat.
  • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent and golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala and it thickens.
  • Add red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, kasuri methi, and salt. Stir well and cook for a few minutes until the spices are well-incorporated.
  • Lower the heat and add the marinated paneer along with any remaining marinade. Stir gently to coat the paneer with the masala.
  • Pour in the heavy cream and mix it well with the masala. Adjust the consistency of the gravy by adding water if needed. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until the paneer is heated through and the flavors meld together.
  • Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve the Paneer Masala hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
  • Enjoy your homemade Paneer Masala, a rich and creamy North Indian dish!
